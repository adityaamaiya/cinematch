# Viewer taste profile

Generated from ~470 rated titles (Skip / Timepass / Go For It / Perfection). Fed to the LLM taste
mode as-is; keep it compact. Regenerate by re-analysing `profile.local.json`.

## Core identity

A craft-first viewer with two dominant modes: **dark, tense, morally-grey storytelling** (crime
sagas, investigative thrillers, mind-bending sci-fi) and **warm, sincere, rooted storytelling**
(underdog biopics, slice-of-life Indian comedy, top-tier animation). What consistently fails with
them is the bland middle: formulaic, committee-made, hype-over-substance content. They watch across
English, Hindi, Korean, Japanese, and South Indian cinema, and are fully comfortable with classics
(12 Angry Men, The Godfather I/II, Deewaar, Blade Runner, Terminator all rated Perfection) — era and
language are never barriers; laziness of craft is.

## What they love (Perfection patterns)

- **Twisty, high-concept thrillers with payoff**: Andhadhun, Drishyam 1–2, The Prestige, Shutter
  Island, Predestination, Oldboy, Memories of Murder, Parasite, Gone Girl, Kahaani, Maharaja, Split.
  A tight screenplay that rewards attention is the single strongest predictor of a top rating.
- **Director worship, but never blind**: Nolan (Dark Knight trilogy, Inception, Interstellar,
  The Prestige, Oppenheimer — yet Insomnia is Timepass and Memento/Tenet only Go For It), Fincher
  (Zodiac, Fight Club, Gone Girl, The Social Network, Mindhunter), Villeneuve (Prisoners, Arrival,
  Blade Runner 2049, Dune 1–2), Rajamouli (Baahubali, Magadheera, Eega), Bong Joon-ho / Park
  Chan-wook, Scorsese (Goodfellas, Wolf of Wall Street, Shutter Island), Hirani (3 Idiots, Munna
  Bhai). They rate the film, not the name.
- **Mind-bending sci-fi and time travel**: The Matrix, Terminator 1–2, Edge of Tomorrow, Dark,
  Severance, 12 Monkeys, Loki, Predestination, Interstellar, Dune, Arrival, The Martian, Project
  Hail Mary. Loves a rigorous concept executed with conviction.
- **Prestige crime / investigation TV**: Breaking Bad, Ozark, Peaky Blinders, Narcos, Mindhunter,
  Paatal Lok, The Family Man, Kohrra, Asur, Black Warrant, Black Bird, DAHMER, The Penguin, Money
  Heist, Adolescence. Slow-burn is welcome when tension keeps ratcheting.
- **Grounded Indian biopics, sports and underdog stories**: Sardar Udham, Uri, Sam Bahadur, Chak
  De! India, Swades, Lagaan, Super 30, 12th Fail, Jersey, Bajrangi Bhaijaan, Homebound, Superboys
  of Malegaon, plus Ford v Ferrari, Rush, F1, Whiplash. Sincerity + earned emotion lands hard.
- **Epic mythology and rooted folklore done seriously**: Ramayan (1987), Mahabharat, Ramayana: The
  Legend of Prince Rama, Baahubali both parts, Kantara: Chapter 1, Tumbbad, KGF 1, Vikram, Animal,
  Dhurandhar. Scale with conviction and cultural rootedness works; hollow VFX spectacle doesn't.
- **Animation at its peak**: both Spider-Verse films, Arcane, WALL-E, Up, Coco, Soul, Ratatouille,
  Kung Fu Panda, How to Train Your Dragon 1–2 + Hidden World, Puss in Boots: The Last Wish, The
  Wild Robot, Klaus, Megamind, The Lego Movie, Big Hero 6. Also serious anime: Attack on Titan,
  Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood, Death Note, Your Name, Spirited Away, Blue Eye Samurai.
- **2000s Hindi ensemble comedy as comfort canon**: Hera Pheri 1–2, Welcome, Golmaal, Mujhse Shaadi
  Karogi, Phir Hera Pheri, Bhool Bhulaiyaa, Jaan-E-Mann, Awara Paagal Deewana — plus TVF-style warm
  slice-of-life (Gullak, TVF Pitchers, Maamla Legal Hai, Sapne vs Everyone, Ted Lasso, The Bear).
- **Dark character studies**: Joker, American Psycho, Nightcrawler-adjacent fare, Taxi Driver
  (liked), Whiplash, Drive, La La Land (musical, but craft-driven emotion).
- **Superhero peaks only**: The Dark Knight, The Batman, Watchmen (film and show), Invincible,
  X-Men '97, Daredevil, The Punisher, Moon Knight, Infinity War/Endgame, Winter Soldier, GotG 1
  and 3, Raimi Spider-Man, No Way Home, Deadpool, Zack Snyder's Justice League. Superhero is a
  delivery mechanism, not a genre they'll forgive.

## What turns them off (Skip / Timepass patterns)

- **Franchise filler and cash-grab sequels**: She-Hulk, Secret Invasion, The Marvels, Echo, Captain
  Marvel, Black Widow, Thor: Love and Thunder (Skip), Ant-Man sequels, Eternals-era MCU filler,
  Despicable Me 4, Kung Fu Panda 4, The Garfield Movie, Mufasa, Lightyear, Ice Age: Buck Wild
  (Skip). Existing IP milked without a reason to exist is their most reliable low rating.
- **Glossy romance / rom-com**: Cocktail, Dil Chahta Hai, Ladies vs Ricky Bahl, Chand Mera Dil —
  all Timepass. Romance only lands when carried by craft or music (La La Land, Your Name, Barfi!,
  Tamasha are Perfection; Raanjhanaa, Gully Boy are Go For It).
- **Hype-over-substance spectacle**: War 2, Brahmāstra, Simmba, Jodhaa Akbar, Kalki-adjacent star
  vehicles rated Timepass; Marco and Anaconda (2025) Skip. Scale without a screenplay bores them.
- **Awards buzz alone doesn't sell them**: Barbie and Anora are Timepass; Slumdog Millionaire is an
  outright Skip (an outsider's-lens take on India that clearly grated). They form their own verdicts.
- **Horror for horror's sake is mid**: Munjya, Bhooth Bangla, Happy Death Day 2U rated Timepass —
  but horror with folklore or craft (Tumbbad, Bhool Bhulaiyaa, The Conjuring, Stree, Heretic,
  Nosferatu) rates well. Pure jump-scare or kiddie horror is a mismatch.
- **Bland kids-only animation**: The Croods spin-offs, I Am Groot, Super Mario Bros. Movie —
  Timepass. Animation must have craft/emotional weight, not just be animated.

## How to judge a new title for them

Weigh **screenplay tightness, directorial conviction, and sincerity** far above genre labels, star
power, or franchise membership. A mid-budget Malayalam survival thriller (Manjummel Boys,
Aadujeevitham) outranks a tentpole with triple the budget. Predict **strong** when a film matches
their loves: twisty thriller, rigorous sci-fi, prestige crime, sincere underdog/biopic, rooted epic,
peak animation, or 2000s-style Hindi ensemble comedy. Predict **mismatch** when it matches their
turn-offs: franchise filler, glossy romance, hollow spectacle, or content coasting on IP/buzz. When
a big franchise entry is genuinely well-crafted, they will still love it — judge the craft, not the
logo.
